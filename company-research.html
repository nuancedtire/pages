How Heidi fits together

Heidi is moving from ambient documentation into a broader “AI Care Partner”: documentation, evidence, communications and purpose-built capture hardware.

Interview takeaway — Show that you understand Heidi as a production clinical workflow company, not just an AI note-taking app.

Product map

1 · Scribe core workflow

Ambient clinical documentation: transcribes/structures consultations into notes and downstream documents. Your angle: documentation burden, real-time capture quality, clinician review and EHR fit.

2 · Evidence point-of-care research

Medical evidence/research retrieval at the point of care. Your angle: source quality, recency, citations, hallucination boundaries and clinical safety evaluation.

3 · Comms beyond the note

Patient communications across calls, bookings, reminders and follow-ups. Your angle: automation needs careful workflow ownership and exception handling.

4 · Remote device / audio layer

Purpose-built wearable microphone: lightweight, offline-capable, encrypted, designed for noisy and mobile clinical settings. Your ED angle is especially relevant here.

Signals to mention

NHS

UK traction matters. Mention that you are interested in how Heidi learns from NHS deployments and converts real clinician feedback into shipped improvements.

ASR

The role mentions voice AI/ASR. Be honest that your strongest direct work is LLM/RAG/full-stack, then pivot to your understanding of noisy clinical environments.
